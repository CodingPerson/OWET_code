/LOCATION
A broad category for physical places or areas that are not defined by political boundaries . Includes natural and man-made geographic features .

/ORGANIZATION
A group of people structured for a specific purpose , such as business , governance , religion , or other collective activities .

/PERSON
An individual human being , identified by name or other distinguishing characteristics .

/SUBSTANCE
A material with a distinct physical presence , which can be natural or synthetic , including chemicals , drugs , and foods .

/GPE
A location defined by political boundaries and governance , such as countries , states , or cities .

/PRODUCT
A tangible item or artifact created , manufactured , or produced for use or sale .

/FACILITIES
Physical structures or complexes built for specific functions , such as buildings , transportation hubs , or attractions .

/DISEASE
A specific medical condition or illness affecting living organisms .

/EVENT
A significant occurrence or happening , often with a specific time and place , such as natural disasters or conflicts .

/GAME
A structured form of play or competition , governed by rules , often for entertainment or sport .

/LANGUAGE
A system of communication , spoken or written , used by a particular community or country .

/PLANT
A living organism belonging to the kingdom Plantae , including trees , flowers , grasses , and other vegetation .

/CONTACT_INFO
Information used to communicate with a person or organization , such as phone numbers , email addresses , or physical addresses .

/WORK_OF_ART
A creative work produced by human skill and imagination , such as literature , music , or visual art .

/LAW
A rule or set of rules established by authority , governing conduct within a community or country .

/ANIMAL
A living organism belonging to the kingdom Animalia , distinct from plants , fungi , and microorganisms .

/LOCATION/REGION
A large , often loosely defined area of land that may span multiple countries or administrative divisions , not necessarily aligned with political boundaries (e.g. , the Middle East , the Arctic) .

/LOCATION/CONTINENT
One of the Earth's principal large landmasses , such as Africa , Asia , or Europe .

/LOCATION/LAKE_SEA_OCEAN
A significant body of water , including lakes (inland) , seas (partially enclosed by land) , and oceans (vast , continuous bodies of salt water) .

/LOCATION/RIVER
A natural flowing watercourse , usually freshwater , moving towards an ocean , sea , lake , or another river .

/ORGANIZATION/CORPORATION
A legally recognized business entity engaged in commercial , industrial , or professional activities .

/ORGANIZATION/GOVERNMENT
An administrative body or institution responsible for governing a country , state , or community .

/ORGANIZATION/POLITICAL
A group formed to influence or participate in political processes , such as political parties or advocacy groups .

/ORGANIZATION/HOTEL
An establishment providing lodging , and often other services , to travelers and guests .

/ORGANIZATION/RELIGIOUS
An institution or group organized around religious beliefs , practices , or worship .

/ORGANIZATION/HOSPITAL
A healthcare institution providing patient treatment with specialized staff and equipment .

/ORGANIZATION/EDUCATIONAL
An institution dedicated to education and learning , such as schools , colleges , or universities .

/SUBSTANCE/DRUG
A chemical substance used for medical treatment , prevention , or diagnosis of disease , or for recreational purposes .

/SUBSTANCE/CHEMICAL
A pure substance or compound with a specific chemical composition , not limited to drugs (e.g. , water , sodium chloride) .

/SUBSTANCE/FOOD
Any substance consumed to provide nutritional support for an organism , including both raw and prepared items .

/GPE/CITY
An urban area with defined boundaries and local governance , typically larger than a town .

/GPE/STATE_PROVINCE
A primary administrative division within a country , such as a state in the USA or a province in Canada .

/GPE/COUNTRY
A sovereign nation with defined borders and its own government .

/PRODUCT/VEHICLE
A manufactured means of transportation , such as cars , airplanes , ships , or trains .

/PRODUCT/WEAPON
An object or device designed for use in inflicting harm or damage , such as firearms , explosives , or knives .

/FACILITIES/BUILDING
A constructed structure with a roof and walls , such as houses , offices , or factories .

/FACILITIES/AIRPORT
A complex for the arrival , departure , and maintenance of aircraft , including runways and terminals .

/FACILITIES/HIGHWAY_STREET
A constructed route for vehicular or pedestrian traffic , including highways , streets , and roads .

/FACILITIES/ATTRACTION
A place or structure designed to attract visitors for leisure , entertainment , or cultural interest , such as amusement parks or landmarks .

/FACILITIES/BRIDGE
A structure built to span a physical obstacle , such as a river or road , for the purpose of providing passage .

/EVENT/HURRICANE
A specific type of natural disaster characterized by a large , powerful tropical cyclone with strong winds and heavy rain .

/EVENT/WAR
A prolonged conflict between countries or groups , typically involving organized armed forces .

/WORK_OF_ART/BOOK
A written or printed literary work , typically bound and consisting of pages .

/WORK_OF_ART/PLAY
A dramatic composition intended for theatrical performance .

/WORK_OF_ART/SONG
A musical composition with lyrics , intended to be sung .
